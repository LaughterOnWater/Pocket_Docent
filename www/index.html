About

Our Mission

Our mission is to help the community understand our place in and reliance upon natural systems through interpretive education and the use of Georgia's native wildlife.

Funding

Bear Hollow relies heavily upon donations and the commitment of many volunteers. If you are interested in volunteering or helping the zoo in any way, please contact us! bearhollowzoo@gmail.com

About the Zoo

Bear Hollow is a unique part of Memorial Park - a facility of the Athens-Clark County Department of Leisure Services. Since it's creation in the late 1940's, the zoo has undergone many changes having started as a private collection of wild animals, which had been donated to the Athens zoo. Currently, the zoo contains only non-releasable native wildlife housed in natural habitat exhibits. The zoo is always growing and changing thanks to community support through volunteers and donations,yet it always remains committed to conservation through the education of its visitors. By educating the public, by allowing even one person to forge a lasting connection with an animal, Bear Hollow helps motivate people to protect wildlife and the habitats that they depend upon.

About this App

I call this app "Pocket Docent". A docent is an official, vetted and trained volunteer who explains or teaches about exhibits in a zoo, museum or public garden setting. They also give general directions to offices, gift shops, restrooms and parking lots, etc.

I think of this app as a digital docent — an app that can answer general questions visitors may have about our animals, zoo hours and the grounds.

I prefer the term "Pocket Docent" because it's less sterile or clinical-sounding than "Digital Docent". While some may think there are plenty of apps out there for specific zoos or public gardens, there are not. This is something relatively new in the app arena. (Surprise!)

This app would have been impossible without the help of the following:

  • Sherrie Hines: She condensed all the animal information from many resources.
  • Wade Sheldon: Most if not all of the animal photos are Wade's photography.

FAQ

Learn a little bit about why we do what we do at the zoo...

How can I volunteer?

Get in touch with Jenny Kvapil. Use the contact information provided on the contact page. Volunteer opportunities include Animal Care, Docent, General Volunteer, and Trail Guide. Volunteers go through a lot of training before they begin helping out. While you don't have to be an adult to become a volunteer, any underage volunteers must be accompanied by a parent or legal guardian while performing their duties. There are many kinds of volunteers. Call or email Jenny to find out how you can start soon!

Why don't the eagles fly away?

Most of our large birds of prey (raptors) have been victims of accidents that have somehow damaged a wing or an eye. Both eagles have wing damage, so they cannot fly to hunt. We are honored to host them as ambassadors of our natural environment so that our visitors can learn more about how animals like eagles play a part in Georgia's wilderness.

Where is the restroom?

The restroom is outside the zoo at the front of Memorial Park's public pool. There is also a restroom to the left of the large parking lot near the playground. Click the "map" button to the bottom right for more information.

How can I have a Zoo Birthday Party?

Make your next celebration a wild affair! Our three-hour zoo parties include use of our Zoo Operations Building, a custom zoo tour and an educational animal program with an animal encounter.

Parties are available:

  • Saturdays: 10:00 a.m. - 1:00 p.m. and 2:00 - 5:00 p.m.
  • Sundays: 2:00 - 5:00 p.m.

Please note that due to space limitations and Zoo Summer Camp, zoo parties are not available during the months of June and July. Also, please book early as spaces fill up quickly!

Reservations must be made at least 3 weeks prior to the date of your party and are taken on a first-come, first-served basis.

Basic Party Package — $75
Includes use of the party room for three hours with tables and chairs and an educational zoo tour and program for the group.

Deluxe Party Package — $125
Includes basic package plus an animal-shaped specialty cake of your choice (serves 20-25), table decorations, plates, cups and party favors for each child.

There is a maximum of 30 participants per party. Please invite no more than 30 guests, including children and adults.

Contact Bear Hollow Zoo about Birthday Parties.

What's all this other activity around the Zoo?

Bear Hollow Zoo is part of Memorial Park. Memorial Park also has a public pool, the Athens Creative Theater, and nature trails, picnic areas, a playground, a public dog park, grills, and all sorts of programming. Check it out here.
