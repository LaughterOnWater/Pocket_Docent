Opossums in Athens

You've probably seen or heard Virginia Opossums at night. They are excellent scavengers and often live near humans to take advantage of our leavings, for instance outdoor pet food bowls. You can learn more than the casual observer by clicking the buttons below...

Taxonomy

Order Didelphimorphia
Family Didelphidae
Genus Didelphis
Species virginiana

Diet

  • In the wild, opossums are omnivorous and will eat anything. Carrion and persimmons are favorites. Insects, other fruits and vegetables, pet food and garbage are also consumed.
  • In captivity, their main diet is composed of a mix of dry cat food, bananas, strawberries and yogurt. Nearly anything can be offered in addition.  

Range and Habitat

  • Originally native to the eastern United States, opossums are now found all throughout the West and up into Canada.
  • In Georgia, they occur statewide.
  • Opossums are cosmopolitan, and can be found in every type of habitat. From forested areas, to mountains, in swamps, and along costal waterways, as well urban areas.

Physical Features

  • Opossums have a long snout on a big round head, with white fur on the face.
  • Ears are black at the base, and pink or white at the tips.
  • The body is covered in thick, but soft, gray fur. Long silver guard hairs cover the undercoat.
  • The tail is long and hairless. Overall, opossums are reminiscent of oversized rats.

Life Span

  • Considerably shorter than comparably sized small mammals. Opossums generally do not live longer than 2 to 4 years.

Reproduction

  • Can occur anytime of the year, most commonly in the spring.
  • Up to 15 young are born in a very premature state, and must crawl blindly into the pouch where they attach to a teat. At this size, they are around the size of a bean.
  • After growing inside the pouch for several weeks, as the young age, they begin to ride piggy-back on their mothers’ fur. They are weaned at about 4 months of age.

Social Behavior

  • Opossums are normally solitary creatures.
  • Active predominantly at night, but may be seen moving around during dusk and dawn.
  • Females may be found with young until they are weaned; even then some young may follow their mother for a period afterward.
  • Nomadic in nature, opossums wander throughout their home ranges foraging for food.

Conservation

  • A species of little conservation concern. Opossums are incredibly common, almost to the point of being considered nuisance animals in some locations.
  • Formerly used as a source of meat of human consumption, but this practice has fallen out of favor.

 Interpretive Information

  • Opossums have a remarkable immune system. They are at least partially, if not completely, immune to venom from snakebite.
  • Not very likely to carry rabies. As few as 1 in 800 opossums are affected by this disease.
  • Opossums have the most teeth of any animal in North America – 52 teeth are in that tiny mouth.
